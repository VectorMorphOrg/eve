# Overview

The Archive preserves historical versions of specifications,
deprecated documents, superseded guidance, and legacy
engineering records from the Evolutionary Virtual Engineer
(E.V.E.) platform.

Archived documents are retained to preserve engineering
history and provide traceability for previous design
decisions.

Documents within this part are **not** considered active
requirements.

---

# Purpose

Part XIV exists to preserve the historical evolution of
EVE-SPEC.

Topics include:

- Superseded Specifications
- Deprecated Documents
- Historical Implementations
- Legacy Design Proposals
- Archived ADRs
- Retired Reference Catalogs
- Obsolete Developer Guides
- Previous Specification Versions

Archived documents provide historical context and should not
be used as the basis for new development.

# Archive Policy

Documents are archived when they are:

- Superseded by newer specifications
- Deprecated
- Replaced
- No longer maintained
- Retained for historical reference

Archived documents shall remain accessible whenever
practical.

---

# Engineering Principles

The Archive follows these principles.

- Historical Accuracy
- Traceability
- Preservation
- Transparency
- Version Awareness
- Non-Destructive Documentation

Information should be archived rather than deleted whenever
possible.

---

# Relationship to EVE-SPEC

Part XIV preserves the historical evolution of EVE-SPEC.

Archived documents shall never override active Engineering
Specifications.

Current specifications always take precedence over archived
material.
